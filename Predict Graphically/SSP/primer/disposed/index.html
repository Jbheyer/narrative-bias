In the following visualization you will be asked questions regarding syringe exchange programs

In 2016 24% of opioid overdose deaths had a syringe found at the scene in Marion County Indiana

Pushback on syringe exchange programs often focus around:

  • Increased use
  • Syringes being ‘ditched’ resulting in accidental needle-sticks in the community

Return Rate: Syringe exchange programs offer new syringes in return for ‘used’ ones. The exchange being 1-for-1 or many for many.

Syringe exchange programs provide sharps containers to decrease the number of unproperly disposed used syringes

In 2016 only 18% of used syringes were disposed of properly leaving 82% unaccounted for (Austin, Indiana)
